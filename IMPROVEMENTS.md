## Your Current Situation
- Jekyll site with Minima theme, gray/maroon color scheme
- Vollkorn serif font, responsive design

- Main goal: **Research visibility**

## Top 10 Changes (Priority Order)

### 1. Modernize the Color Palette

**Current**: Gray menu (#8c8c8c), maroon accents (#800000), light gray background (#f2f2f2)

**Recommendation**: Shift to a warmer, more sophisticated palette that feels academic but fresh:
- Background: Warm off-white (#fafaf8) instead of cold gray
- Primary accent: Deep navy (#1a365d) or teal (#0d6e6e) instead of maroon
- Menu: Near-black (#1a1a2e) for contrast
- Links: Teal or navy accent

**Why**: The current gray feels institutional/dated. A refined palette will instantly modernize without losing professionalism.

### 2. Dramatically Improve Typography & Spacing

**Current**: Vollkorn at 1.15em, cramped line-height, inconsistent spacing

**Recommendation**:
- Increase base font size to 17-18px
- Line-height from ~1.4 to 1.6-1.7 for body text
- Add generous whitespace between sections (2-3rem instead of 1rem)
- One serif for headings (Playfair Display), clean sans-serif for body (Inter/Source Sans)
- Paper titles: Larger and bolder

**Why**: Better spacing is the #1 thing that separates amateur and professional design.
